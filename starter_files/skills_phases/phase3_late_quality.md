# Phase III — LATE QUALITY

## When
- 4–8 weeks before the race
- After Phase II, when legs are ready for intensity

## Goal
Maximize VO2max (I-pace) and shift the lactate threshold (T-pace). This is **the hardest phase** of training.

## Weekly structure
- **2× Easy run** (shorter than Phase I/II — recovery between accents matters!)
- **1× Long run** (with M-pace inserts — e.g. 14km with 8km @ M-pace in the middle)
- **1× Quality accent — rotation:**
  - **Intervals (I-pace)** — 4–6× 1km @ I-pace, 2–3 min jog
  - **Threshold/Cruise** — 4–5× 1.5km @ T-pace, 1 min rest
  - **Continuous Tempo** — 20–25 min @ T-pace
  - Short 5km race (once per phase, as a test)

## Daniels' volume rules
- Max T per session: **10% of weekly volume**
- Max I per session: **8% of weekly volume**
- Total I+T+R per week: max 8–10%

## Avoid
- Two accents in a row (always easy/rest between them)
- Ignoring fatigue — Garmin recovery time is a good indicator
- Pushing the last rep "all out" (leads to injury)

## Rotation
- I ↔ T week by week (NEVER 2 weeks in a row of the same!)
- Every 3–4 weeks: recovery week (-25%)

## Progress markers
- T-pace held at lower HR (threshold shifted)
- Negative split in interval workouts
- Race predictors in Garmin/Strava trending down

## Warning signs
- Resting HR rises 2-3 days in a row → REST
- Garmin recovery time >48h → push back the accent
- Joint or tendon pain → STOP

## Typical duration
3–5 weeks before transitioning to Phase IV/Taper
