# Phase III — LATE QUALITY

## When
- After Phase II, when legs are ready for intensity
- See timeline by distance below

## Timeline by distance
| Distance | When | Duration |
|----------|------|----------|
| 5K      | 2–4 weeks before race / virtual test | 2–3 weeks |
| 10K     | 3–6 weeks before race / virtual test | 3–4 weeks |
| HM      | 4–8 weeks before race | 3–5 weeks |
| Maraton | 8–14 weeks before race | 4–6 weeks |

## Goal
Maximize VO2max (I-pace) and shift the lactate threshold (T-pace). **Hardest phase.**
Each distance has a different emphasis — read carefully.

## Weekly structure + quality accent by distance

### 5K — I-pace and R-pace dominant
The 5K is run at roughly I-pace. Train the engine that powers it.
- **2× Easy run** (shorter — recovery matters)
- **1× Long run** (40–55 min, no M-pace inserts needed)
- **1× Quality accent — rotation:**
  - **Intervals:** 5–7× 1km @ I-pace, 2–3 min jog recovery
  - **Reps:** 6–8× 400m @ R-pace, full rest (90–120 sec walk)
  - Short race (1× per phase, parkrun or local 5K)

### 10K — I-pace and T-pace balanced
The 10K sits between I and T pace zones. Train both.
- **2× Easy run**
- **1× Long run** (55–70 min, optional 10 min M-pace insert)
- **1× Quality accent — rotation:**
  - **Intervals:** 4–6× 1km @ I-pace, 2–3 min jog
  - **Threshold/Cruise:** 4–5× 1.5km @ T-pace, 1 min rest
  - **Continuous Tempo:** 20–25 min @ T-pace
  - Short race (1× per phase, 5K as test)

### HM — T-pace and I-pace balanced
Half marathon is run ~10–15 sec/km faster than T-pace. Threshold work is king.
- **2× Easy run** (shorter than Phase I/II)
- **1× Long run** (with M-pace inserts — e.g. 14km with 8km @ M-pace in middle)
- **1× Quality accent — rotation:**
  - **Threshold/Cruise:** 4–5× 1.5km @ T-pace, 1 min rest
  - **Continuous Tempo:** 20–25 min @ T-pace
  - **Intervals:** 4–6× 1km @ I-pace, 2–3 min jog
  - Short 5K or 10K race (1× per phase)

### Marathon — T-pace and long M-pace runs
Marathon is run at M-pace. Long runs with sustained M-pace are the key.
- **2× Easy run**
- **1× Long run** (22–30km, with 12–16km @ M-pace in second half)
- **1× Quality accent — rotation:**
  - **Continuous Tempo:** 30–35 min @ T-pace
  - **Cruise intervals:** 5× 1.5km @ T-pace, 1 min rest
  - **M-pace progression:** 10km easy → 10km @ M-pace

## Daniels' volume rules
- Max T per session: **10% of weekly volume**
- Max I per session: **8% of weekly volume**
- Total I+T+R per week: max 8–10%

## Avoid
- Two quality sessions in a row (always easy/rest between them)
- Ignoring fatigue — Garmin recovery time is a reliable indicator
- Pushing the last rep "all out" (leads to injury)

## Rotation
- I ↔ T week by week (NEVER 2 weeks in a row of the same type)
- Every 3–4 weeks: recovery week (-25%)

## Progress markers
- T-pace held at lower HR (threshold shifted)
- Negative split in interval workouts
- Race predictors in Garmin/Strava trending down

## Warning signs
- Resting HR rises 2–3 days in a row → REST
- Garmin recovery time >48h → push back the accent
- Joint or tendon pain → STOP
