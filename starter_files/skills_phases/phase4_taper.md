# Phase IV — TAPER (Final + last 2 weeks)

## When
- Last 10–14 days before the race (HM)
- For marathon: 2–3 weeks

## Goal
**Sharpen form.** Reduce fatigue while maintaining intensity. Form rises 7–10 days after the last hard stimulus.

## Golden rule of taper
**Reduce VOLUME, not INTENSITY.** Shorten runs but T-pace must still appear (briefly).

## Last 10 days structure (HM)
```
Day -10: last medium-long or tempo (last hard stimulus)
Day  -9: easy
Day  -8: REST
Day  -7: last short accent (3×1km T-pace) or easy + strides
Days -6 to -3: easy or REST + strides (NEVER 4 days REST in a row!)
Day  -2: REST or 20 min very easy
Day  -1: SHAKEOUT (the only one!) 15-20min + 4× strides + optional 1× 1min @ race pace
Day   0: RACE
```

## SHAKEOUT — details
**ONE shakeout, day before race.** Goal: activation, not fatigue.

Ideal structure:
- 15–20 min easy @ E+1 pace (slower than usual easy)
- 3–5× 100m strides (relaxed, full rest)
- **+1× 1 minute @ race pace** (pace reminder, activation)

If traveling: do shakeout at the race location (familiarization, course check).

## Avoid during taper
- **4 full REST days in a row** — legs "fall asleep". Insert 1× easy + strides in the middle.
- New shoes (test earlier!)
- New foods the day before
- Standing on legs day -1 (sightseeing, queues, etc.)
- Trying "one last test" workout in race week

## Mental side of taper
"Taper madness" — typical feelings:
- Legs feel heavy/strange — this is NORMAL
- Doubts "am I ready" — answer by looking at forma.md
- Urge to "squeeze in" one more workout — DO NOT do it

## Markers of good taper
- Resting HR stable or lower
- Better sleep on days -3 to -1
- Energy rising over the last 3 days
- Garmin Body Battery high in the morning of day -1

## What to EAT
- Days -3 to -1: increase carbs to 60–70% of diet
- Day -1: light dinner, familiar food, 3-4h before sleep
- Day 0 morning: 2-3h before start, 80-100g carbs + coffee

## What to DRINK
- Consistent hydration all week
- Day -1: ~3L water/electrolytes (light yellow urine = OK)
- Pre-race: 500ml 60-90 min before
