# Phase IV — TAPER (Final + Race Week)

## When
- Final days before race or virtual test
- Taper length varies by distance — start counting back from race day

## Taper length by distance
| Dystans | Taper start | Charakter |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| 5K      | Day -5 to -7 | Krótki, głównie łatwe biegi + strides |
| 10K     | Day -7       | Tydzień zmniejszonej objętości |
| HM      | Day -10      | Klasyczny taper z ostatnim akcentem D-7 |
| Maraton | Day -14 to -21 | Długi taper, stopniowe zmniejszanie |

## Golden rule
**Reduce VOLUME, not INTENSITY.** Shorten runs, but T-pace must still appear briefly.
Form rises 7–10 days after the last hard stimulus.

---

## 5K Taper (5–7 days)
```
Day -6/-7: last quality session (4–6× 400m @ R-pace or 3× 1km @ I-pace)
Day    -5: easy 30 min
Day    -4: easy 25 min + 4× strides
Day    -3: REST or 20 min very easy
Day    -2: easy 20 min + 4× strides
Day    -1: SHAKEOUT — 15 min easy + 3–4× strides + 1× 1 min @ race pace
Day     0: RACE
```

## 10K Taper (7 days)
```
Day  -7: last quality session (3× 1km @ T-pace or 3× 1km @ I-pace)
Day  -6: easy 35 min
Day  -5: easy 30 min + 4× strides
Day  -4: REST
Day  -3: easy 25 min + 4× strides
Day  -2: REST or 20 min very easy
Day  -1: SHAKEOUT — 15–20 min easy + 4× strides + 1× 1 min @ race pace
Day   0: RACE
```

## HM Taper (10 days)
```
Day -10: last medium-long or tempo (last hard stimulus)
Day  -9: easy
Day  -8: REST
Day  -7: last short accent (3× 1km T-pace) or easy + strides
Days -6 to -3: easy or REST + strides (NEVER 4 days REST in a row!)
Day  -2: REST or 20 min very easy
Day  -1: SHAKEOUT — 15–20 min easy + 4× strides + 1× 1 min @ race pace
Day   0: RACE
```

## Maraton Taper (14–21 days)
```
Day -21 to -15: last long run (32–34km), then volume drops 25%
Day -14: last quality session (4× 1.5km @ T-pace)
Day -13: easy 50 min
Day -12: REST
Day -11 to -8: easy 40–50 min + strides, one day REST
Day  -7: easy 35 min + 6× strides
Day  -6: easy 30 min
Day  -5: REST
Day  -4: easy 30 min + 4× strides + 2× 1km @ M-pace
Day  -3: easy 25 min
Day  -2: REST or 20 min very easy
Day  -1: SHAKEOUT — 15–20 min easy + 4× strides + 1× 1 min @ race pace
Day   0: RACE
```

---

## SHAKEOUT — details
**ONE shakeout, day before race.** Goal: activation, not fatigue.

Ideal structure:
- 15–20 min easy @ E+1 pace (slower than usual easy)
- 3–5× 100m strides (relaxed, full rest)
- **+1× 1 minute @ race pace** (pace reminder, activation)

If traveling: do shakeout at the race location (familiarization, course check).

---

## Virtual test taper (fitness mode — no race)
Same structure as the distance you're testing.
Treat the time trial as a race: same warm-up, same shakeout the day before.
After the test → record result in fitness.md → update VDOT → start next training cycle.

---

## Avoid during taper
- **4 full REST days in a row** — legs "fall asleep". Insert 1× easy + strides in the middle.
- New shoes (test at least 2 weeks before)
- New foods the day before
- Standing on legs all day before the race (sightseeing, queues)
- "One last test" workout in race week

## Mental side of taper
"Taper madness" — typical feelings:
- Legs feel heavy/strange — **this is NORMAL**
- Doubts "am I ready" — look at fitness.md and your training log
- Urge to "squeeze in" one more workout — **do not do it**

## Markers of good taper
- Resting HR stable or lower
- Better sleep on days -3 to -1
- Energy rising over the last 3 days
- Garmin Body Battery high in the morning of day -1

## Nutrition
- Days -3 to -1: increase carbs to 60–70% of diet
- Day -1: light dinner, familiar food, 3–4h before sleep
- Day 0 morning: 2–3h before start, 80–100g carbs + coffee

## Hydration
- Consistent hydration all week
- Day -1: ~3L water/electrolytes (light yellow urine = OK)
- Pre-race: 500ml 60–90 min before start
