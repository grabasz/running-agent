# Phase I — BASE (Foundation)

## When
- Beginning of preparation block (see timeline by distance below)
- After Phase 0 graduation (beginner)
- After a break/injury
- After ending the previous season

## Goal
Build aerobic endurance and running resilience. **Volume matters more than intensity.**

## Timeline by distance
| Dystans | Kiedy zacząć | Czas trwania fazy |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| 5K      | 8–12 weeks before race / virtual test | 3–4 weeks |
| 10K     | 12–16 weeks before race / virtual test | 4–6 weeks |
| HM      | 16–24 weeks before race | 4–8 weeks |
| Maraton | 24–32 weeks before race | 8–12 weeks |

## Weekly structure by distance

### 5K / 10K
- **2× Easy run** (25–45 min @ E-pace)
- **1× Long run** (40–60 min, up to 25% of weekly volume)
- **1× Easy + Strides** (30–40 min easy + 6×100m strides)
- Optional: Hills (6–8×60 sec uphill)

### HM
- **2× Easy run** (30–60 min @ E-pace)
- **1× Long run** (60–90 min, up to 25–30% of weekly volume)
- **1× Easy + Strides** (45 min easy + 6–8×100m strides)
- Optional: Hills (8–10×60–90 sec uphill, jog down)

### Maraton
- **2× Easy run** (45–75 min @ E-pace)
- **1× Long run** (90–120 min, up to 30% of weekly volume)
- **1× Easy + Strides** (60 min easy + 6–8×100m strides)
- Optional: Hills (8–10×90 sec uphill)

## Avoid
- Continuous T-pace or I-pace work
- Races (except as a test, max 1 per phase)
- Increasing volume by >10% week over week (10% rule)

## Rotation
- Hills and Strides alternate weekly
- Every 3–4 weeks: recovery week (-20–30% volume)

## Progress markers
- HR at easy pace drops at the same speed
- Long run extends without fatigue
- No need for forced rest days between runs
