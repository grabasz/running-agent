# Phase I — BASE (Foundation)

## When
- Beginning of preparation for a goal race (>12 weeks out)
- After a break/injury
- After ending the previous season, before starting a new block

## Goal
Build aerobic endurance and running resilience. **Volume matters more than intensity.**

## Weekly structure
- **2× Easy run** (30–60 min @ E-pace)
- **1× Long run** (60–90 min, up to 25–30% of weekly volume)
- **1× Easy + Strides** (45 min easy + 6–8×100m strides)
- Optional: 1× Hills (8–10×60–90 sec uphill, jog down)

## Avoid
- Continuous T-pace or I-pace work
- Races (except as a test, max 1 per phase)
- Increasing volume by >10% week over week (10% rule)

## Rotation
- Hills and Strides alternate weekly
- Every 4 weeks: recovery week (-20–30% volume)

## Progress markers
- HR at easy pace drops at the same speed
- Long run extends without fatigue
- No need for forced rest days between runs

## Typical duration
4–8 weeks before transitioning to Phase II
