# SKILLS PLANNING — training structure and planning

> Load when: planning training, building a weekly plan, calculating volume, discussing phases.
> For a specific phase → also load `skills_phases/phaseN_*.md`.

## Training phases (Jack Daniels)
```
Phase 0 — RUN/WALK (beginner only, build to 5K continuous, 8–10 wk)
Phase I  — BASE (Easy + Strides)
Phase II — EARLY QUALITY (R + T/M)
Phase III — LATE QUALITY (I + T)
Phase IV — TAPER (5K: 5–7d | 10K: 7d | HM: 10d | M: 14–21d)
```
Mode from `profile.md`: `beginner` → Phase 0+I | `fitness` → virtual TT → update VDOT | `race_prep` → count back from race date.
**SHAKEOUT = ONE, day before race.** Traveling → at race venue.

## Weekly structure (Daniels + 80/20)
Pattern: 2× Easy + 1× Quality (rotation) + 1× Long.
Recovery week every 3–4 weeks: −20–30% volume, no quality sessions.
Quality rotation: I | T | R+Strides | Hills | Tempo | Fartlek | LongRun+M
VDOT zones: E (aerobic) | M (race economy) | T (lactate clear) | I (VO2max) | R (speed)
Update VDOT after every race: https://runsmartproject.com/calculator/

## Volume calibration (always before building a plan)

### Step 1 — establish baseline (volume_log.md → Strava fallback)
1. Check `volume_log.md` — updated <7 days ago? → read it, zero Strava calls. Average is in the header.
2. Missing / stale → run `/volume` → updates the file.
3. **Baseline = that number. A plan never starts from zero or an arbitrarily low value.**

### Step 2 — volume change rules
| Situation | Change vs baseline |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Normal training week | max +10% week-to-week (10% rule) |
| Week with 2× strength training | −5–10% (total stress stays similar) |
| Recovery week (every 3–4 weeks) | −20–30%, no quality |
| Immediately after HM | −30–40%, easy only |
| Immediately after marathon | −50–60%, walks and recovery |
| HM taper (last 10 days) | −25–35% from block peak |
| Marathon taper (last 21 days) | −30–40% gradually |

Rule: never cut volume abruptly without reason (injury / illness / planned taper).

### Step 3 — strength training does not replace running
- Strength → injury resilience + running economy (especially hills and finish)
- Does **NOT** replace aerobic adaptation from Easy running
- 2× strength/week → reduce planned km by 5–10%, no more
- Full strength (legs + core): after easy runs, **never** the day before a quality session
- Maintenance strength (taper): bodyweight only, no load, after day −10

### Step 4 — Garmin suggestions vs plan
Garmin calculates suggestions from training load — it does not know about your race plan.
In race build-up: **the plan wins, Garmin is ignored.**
Exception: Body Battery <40 in the morning → consider swapping quality for easy or rest day.
